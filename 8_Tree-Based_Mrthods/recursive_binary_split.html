Recursive Binary Splitting

Example Partition & Corresponding Decision Tree

Figure 1 — Two-Dimensional Feature Space Partition

Figure 1. A partition of the two-dimensional feature space (X₁ ∈ [0,10], X₂ ∈ [0,8]) produced by recursive binary splitting. Five cuts — t₁ through t₅ — divide the plane into six rectangular regions R₁ – R₆. The value ŷ inside each region is the mean response for training observations falling there.

Figure 2 — Corresponding Decision Tree

Figure 2. The decision tree corresponding to the partition in Figure 1. Each internal node (dark rectangle) shows a binary split condition. Branches drop vertically from a horizontal crossbar — left = Yes (condition true), right = No (condition false). Each leaf shows the region label, mean prediction ŷ, and the defining inequalities.
